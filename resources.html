Twelve papers carry most of this course. Read them in course order; each one is introduced properly in its week.

Foundations

  • A Neural Probabilistic Language Model Bengio, Ducharme, Vincent, Jauvin · 2003 · Week 1

    Week 1 reenacts this paper; most of what followed builds on its ideas.

  • Neural Machine Translation of Rare Words with Subword Units Sennrich, Haddow, Birch · 2016 · Week 2

    Where BPE tokenization enters modern NLP. You build it from scratch in Week 2.

  • Attention Is All You Need Vaswani et al. · 2017 · Weeks 4–5

    The transformer. Read it after Week 4 and it stops being intimidating.

  • Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners Radford et al. (GPT-2) · 2019 · Week 5

    The Week 5 recipe at a much larger scale.

Training and scale

  • Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models Kaplan et al. · 2020 · Week 6

    Loss falls predictably with compute, data, and parameters. Run-sizing starts here.

  • Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models Hoffmann et al. (Chinchilla) · 2022 · Week 6

    Most models before it were undertrained on data; it changed how runs are sized.

Post-training

  • Training Language Models to Follow Instructions with Human Feedback Ouyang et al. (InstructGPT) · 2022 · Weeks 9 · 11

    The SFT plus RLHF pipeline that turned completion engines into assistants.

  • LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models Hu et al. · 2021 · Week 10

    Fine-tuning in a low-rank subspace. The reason one rented GPU is enough to matter.

  • Direct Preference Optimization Rafailov et al. · 2023 · Week 11

    Preference tuning without a reward model or RL loop. Week 11’s lab runs it.

Inference and serving

  • FlashAttention: Fast and Memory-Efficient Exact Attention with IO-Awareness Dao et al. · 2022 · Week 13

    Attention rebuilt around the memory hierarchy. Week 13 borrows its way of thinking.

  • Efficient Memory Management for Large Language Model Serving with PagedAttention Kwon et al. (vLLM) · 2023 · Week 14

    Virtual memory for the KV cache. The paper behind the serving engine you will use.

  • Fast Inference from Transformers via Speculative Decoding Leviathan, Kalman, Matias · 2023 · Week 15

    A small model drafts, a big model verifies. Week 15 measures the speedup.

  • Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch) Sebastian Raschka · Manning · 2024

    The closest cousin to Phase 1 of these notes: a GPT built end to end in plain PyTorch.

  • Understanding Deep Learning Simon J. D. Prince · MIT Press · free online

    Rigorous and modern; the reference I check when I want the math done properly.

  • Dive into Deep Learning Zhang, Lipton, Li, Smola · free online

    Code-first and runnable everywhere. Good bridge material for Weeks 0 through 6.

  • Deep Learning Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville · MIT Press · free online

    Older but thorough; consult it like an encyclopedia rather than a syllabus.

  • Hands-On Large Language Models Jay Alammar, Maarten Grootendorst · O’Reilly · 2024

    Visual and applied. Strongest on using and adapting models rather than pretraining them.

  • Machine Learning Engineering Open Book Stas Bekman · free, continuously updated

    Battle notes from very large training and inference fleets. The spine weeks lean on this.

  • Hands-On LLM Serving and Optimization: Hosting LLMs at Scale Chi Wang, Peiheng Hu · O’Reilly · 2026

    Deploying and optimizing LLMs at scale: latency, throughput, and cost. The book closest to the Weeks 13–18 core.

  • AI Systems Performance Engineering Chris Fregly · O’Reilly · 2025

    Full-stack performance tuning for training and inference: GPUs, CUDA, and PyTorch. Link goes to the book’s code and labs.

  • The Illustrated Transformer Jay Alammar

    The visual walkthrough most people learn attention from; Week 4 pairs with it.

  • The Annotated Transformer Harvard NLP

    The original paper, annotated line by line with working code.

  • LLM Visualization Brendan Bycroft

    A 3D, step-by-step tour through an actual GPT forward pass. Set aside an hour.

  • Transformer Explainer Polo Club, Georgia Tech

    An interactive GPT-2 running live in your browser, every tensor inspectable.

  • Transformer Inference Arithmetic kipply

    Back-of-envelope math for latency, memory, and cost. Week 13 in blog form.

  • Lil’Log Lilian Weng

    Survey-grade deep dives. When a topic here needs more, her post on it is usually the next stop.

  • Simon Willison’s Weblog Simon Willison

    What is happening in LLMs, tested by hand, several times a week.

  • CS336: Language Modeling from Scratch Stanford · lectures and assignments public

    The closest academic sibling to these notes; a full LLM built across a semester.

  • Neural Networks: Zero to Hero Andrej Karpathy · free video series

    Everything built by hand on video; Weeks 0 through 5 walk beside it.

  • Practical Deep Learning for Coders fast.ai · Jeremy Howard

    Code-first deep learning; a gentler parallel track through Phase 1.

  • Hugging Face LLM Course Hugging Face · free

    Tokenizers, fine-tuning, and the library stack several labs also use.

  • GPU MODE Lectures community lecture series

    GPU programming and kernels; background for the inference weeks.

Podcasts are RSS feeds with audio, so any podcast app finds these by name. All six were publishing new episodes when this page was last verified.

  • Latent Space swyx, Alessio Fanelli

    Interviews with the people building applied AI: inference, agents, evals. Closest to this site’s lane.

  • Dwarkesh Podcast Dwarkesh Patel

    Long interviews where researchers think out loud; pairs well with the training and scaling weeks.

  • Machine Learning Street Talk Tim Scarfe

    Deep technical conversations, often with the paper’s own authors.

  • The TWIML AI Podcast Sam Charrington

    A long-running interview archive; search it by topic when a week sparks something.

  • Practical AI Daniel Whitenack, Chris Benson

    The most approachable of the set; a good companion for Weeks 0 through 8.

  • Gradient Dissent Lukas Biewald, Weights & Biases

    Practitioners on what shipped and what did not; the labs use the same experiment tooling.
